# NLP Entity Schema

| Variable | Type | Range / values | Meaning / notes | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Clinical_Dementia_Rating_Scale_Global_CDR` | Numeric | `0`, `0.5`, `1`, `2`, `3` | Global CDR, not CDR-Sum-of-Boxes. Higher means worse cognitive/functional impairment. Interpretation: `0=normal`, `0.5=very mild dementia`, `1=mild dementia`, `2=moderate dementia`, `3=severe dementia` | [Knight ADRC](https://knightadrc.wustl.edu/professionals-clinicians/cdr-dementia-staging-instrument/cdr-scoring-table/) |
| `Cornell_Scale_For_Depression_In_Dementia` | Numeric | `0-38` | Higher means more depressive symptoms in dementia. The CSDD is a 19-item clinician-administered scale using information from both patient and informant/staff interviews; each item is scored `0=absent`, `1=mild/intermittent`, `2=severe`. Common interpretation: `<6=absence of significant depressive symptoms`, `>10=probable major depression`, `>18=definite major depression` | [Alexopoulos 1988](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3337862/); [CSDD scoring guide](https://www.dementiaresearch.org.au/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/CSDD.pdf) |
| `Geriatric_Depression_Scale` | Numeric | `GDS-15: 0-15`; `GDS-30: 0-30` | Higher means more depressive symptoms. The GDS is a yes/no self-report depression screening scale for older adults; score one point for each depression-indicating answer. Capture the version when possible. Common GDS-15 interpretation: `0-4=normal`, `5-8=mild depression`, `9-11=moderate depression`, `12-15=severe depression`; GDS-30 is commonly interpreted as `0-9=normal`, `10-19=mild depression`, `20-30=severe depression` | [Yesavage 1983](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7183759/); [Stanford GDS](https://web.stanford.edu/~yesavage/GDS.html); [HIGN GDS](https://hign.org/consultgeri/try-this-series/geriatric-depression-scale-gds) |
| `Hachinski_Ischemic_Score` | Numeric | `0-18` | Higher means stronger vascular/ischemic etiology, not dementia severity. Common interpretation: `<=4` favors Alzheimer/primary degenerative dementia, `5-6` is mixed or indeterminate, `>=7` favors vascular or multi-infarct dementia. Capture modified versions separately if the note states one | [Hachinski 1974 (concept)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4135618/); [Hachinski 1975 (13-item scale)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1180496/); [Moroney 1997](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9339696/) |
| `Mini-Mental_State_Examination_Score` | Numeric | `0-30` | Higher means better cognitive performance; lower scores indicate greater cognitive impairment. The MMSE is a 30-point screening measure covering orientation, registration, attention/calculation, recall, language, and visuospatial copying. Common severity interpretation: `>=24=normal cognition`, `19-23=mild cognitive impairment`, `10-18=moderate cognitive impairment`, `<=9=severe cognitive impairment`. Interpretation should be based on raw score and clinical context; it is not a standalone dementia diagnosis | [Folstein 1975](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1202204/); [NCBI Bookshelf](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK575016/) |
| `Montreal_Cognitive_Assessment_Score` | Numeric | `0-30` | Higher means better cognitive performance; lower scores indicate greater impairment. The MoCA is a brief screening tool for mild cognitive impairment and covers short-term memory, visuospatial abilities, executive functions, attention/concentration/working memory, language, and orientation. Education adjustment (official rule): add 1 point if the subject has 12 or fewer years of formal education; maximum score remains 30. MoCA official severity interpretation: `>=26=normal cognitive performance`, `18-25=mild impairment`, `10-17=moderate impairment`, `0-9=severe impairment`; the MoCA FAQ notes the moderate and severe groups were defined by default rather than derived like the normal/MCI ranges | [Nasreddine 2005](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15817019/); [MoCA official](https://mocacognition.com/faq/) |
| `NPI_Total_Score` | Numeric | `NPI-10: 0-120`; `NPI-12: 0-144`; `NPI-Q severity: 0-36` | Higher means greater neuropsychiatric symptom burden. Full NPI domain score: `0` when behavior is absent, or `frequency (1-4) x severity (1-3) = 1-12` when present; total score is summed across 10 or 12 domains. NPI-Q severity is different: each present domain is rated `1=mild`, `2=moderate`, `3=severe`, summed across 12 domains (`0-36`) without frequency. Caregiver distress is scored separately and should not be merged into the total score. There is no single universal severity cutoff separately validated for NPI-10, NPI-12, and NPI-Q. One trial assessment source uses `<20=mild`, `20-<50=moderate`, `>=50=severe` for 12-domain full NPI total symptom severity, but this should not be applied to NPI-Q or treated as a universal cutoff | [Cummings 1994](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7991117/); [NPI scoring guide](https://www.dementiaresearch.org.au/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/NPI.pdf); [NPI-Q](https://alz.org/getmedia/fac45de4-c45d-4227-8e32-1153b4cdf6f3/npiq-questionnaire.pdf); [NPI review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9108559/); [NCBI Bookshelf](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK575016/) |
| `Education_Years` | Numeric | integer years, often `0-30+` | Years of formal education completed |  |
| `Smoking` | Categorical / Numeric | see `Smoking Subschema` below | Smoking status and quantitative smoking history, including status, pack-years, packs per day, smoking duration, and quit time | [Yang 2020](https://doi.org/10.1109/ICHI48887.2020.9374369) |
| `Mattis Dementia Rating Scale` | Numeric | `0-144` | Higher means better cognitive performance; lower scores indicate greater impairment. The DRS-2 assesses overall cognitive functioning in adults with cognitive impairment and includes five subscales: Attention, Initiation/Perseveration, Construction, Conceptualization, and Memory. Interpret using raw score, version, and age/education-adjusted norms. There is no single universal severity cutoff; published thresholds are population-specific, such as Parkinson disease studies using `<=140/144` for PD-MCI and `<=132/144` for Parkinson disease dementia | [PAR DRS-2](https://www.parinc.com/products/DRS-2); [PAR sample report](https://www.parinc.com/docs/default-source/product-resources/sample_drs2.pdf?sfvrsn=e4774a0c_4); [Matteau 2012](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22689702/) |
| `Global_Deterioration_Scale` | Numeric | stage `1-7` | Higher stage means greater cognitive/functional deterioration in primary degenerative dementia. Interpretation: `1=no cognitive decline`, `2=very mild cognitive decline`, `3=mild cognitive decline`, `4=moderate cognitive decline`, `5=moderately severe cognitive decline`, `6=severe cognitive decline`, `7=very severe cognitive decline` | [Reisberg 1982](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7114305/) |
| `Telephone_Interview_of_Cognitive_Status_score` | Numeric | `TICS: 0-41`; modified versions may differ | Higher means better cognitive performance; lower scores indicate greater impairment. The TICS is a brief standardized cognitive status assessment designed for telephone administration when in-person screening is impractical; it may also be administered face-to-face and is useful when visual, reading, or writing limitations affect testing. It should not be used alone to diagnose a specific neurological or psychiatric disorder. Capture version/denominator when stated, because modified forms may use different ranges | [PAR TICS](https://www.parinc.com/products/TICS); [Brandt 1988](https://pure.johnshopkins.edu/en/publications/the-telephone-interview-for-cognitive-status-3/); [Ann Fam Med 2022](https://www.annfammed.org/content/annalsfm/20/2/130.full.pdf) |

## Smoking Subschema

| Entity | Full name | Type | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `SMOKING_STATUS` | Smoking status | Categorical | Current, former, never, or unknown smoking status | `current smoker`; `former smoker`; `never smoked`; `denies tobacco use` |
| `PACK_YEAR` | Pack-year | Numeric exposure | Total cumulative smoking exposure; `1 pack-year = 1 pack per day x 1 year` | `30 pack-year history` |
| `PACK_PER_DAY` | Pack per day | Numeric amount | Number of packs smoked per day | `1 pack per day`; `0.5 ppd`; `two packs daily` |
| `SMOKING_DURATION` | Smoking year | Duration / Numeric | Duration of smoking in years | `smoked for 40 years`; `20-year smoking history` |
| `QUIT_TIME` | Quit year | Temporal / Numeric | Time of smoking cessation, including calendar year, age at quit, or relative quit time | `quit in 2015`; `quit at age 55`; `quit 10 years ago` |
